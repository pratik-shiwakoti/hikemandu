Nagarkot Sunrise Trek

4-5 hours Easy to Moderate 8 km round trip East of Kathmandu

Trail Overview

4.0 (96 reviews)

Nagarkot is famous for its stunning sunrise views over the Himalayan range. This trek takes you through charming villages, terraced fields, and pine forests to reach the perfect viewpoint for witnessing a spectacular sunrise over the mountains.

Located just 32 km east of Kathmandu, Nagarkot sits at an elevation of 2,175 meters and offers panoramic views of the Himalayas including Mount Everest on clear days. The trail is relatively easy and suitable for beginners, making it a perfect weekend getaway from the city.

Trail Highlights

  • Breathtaking sunrise views over the Himalayas
  • Panoramic mountain vistas including Everest (on clear days)
  • Traditional Tamang villages along the route
  • Lush forests and terraced farmlands
  • Easy accessibility from Kathmandu

Trail Information

  • Difficulty Easy to Moderate
  • Duration 4-5 hours
  • Distance 8 km round trip
  • Elevation Gain 500 meters
  • Best Season October to May
  • Starting Point Nagarkot Bus Stop
  • Entrance Fee None

Detailed Trail Description

1 Starting Point: Nagarkot Bus Stop

The trek begins at the Nagarkot bus stop. From here, follow the main road toward the Nagarkot view tower. This is a good place to start early in the morning before sunrise.

2 Nagarkot View Tower

A short walk from the bus stop brings you to the famous Nagarkot view tower, which offers 360-degree panoramic views of the Himalayas. This is an excellent spot to watch the sunrise if you're starting your trek early.

3 Trail to Tamang Village

From the view tower, follow the trail that descends through pine forests. The path is well-marked and takes you through beautiful scenery. After about an hour, you'll reach a traditional Tamang village where you can experience local culture.

4 Terraced Fields and Forest Path

Continue your trek through terraced fields and more forest areas. This section offers beautiful views of the surrounding countryside and distant mountains. The trail is mostly downhill or flat in this section.

5 Return Journey

The return journey follows the same path back to Nagarkot, but with a steady uphill climb. Take your time and enjoy the different perspective of the landscape as you ascend. The total round trip takes about 4-5 hours at a leisurely pace.

Essential Tips

  • Start early (around 5 AM) to catch the sunrise
  • Bring warm clothes as mornings can be chilly
  • Carry at least 1 liter of water per person
  • Wear comfortable hiking shoes with good grip
  • Bring a camera for the spectacular views
  • Consider staying overnight in Nagarkot for the best sunrise experience
  • Check weather forecasts for clear viewing conditions
  • Bring some cash for refreshments in local shops
